Bacterial Blight Disease Information

Overview

Bacterial blight, caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas citri subsp. malvacearum, is a serious disease affecting cotton. It targets all aerial parts and can drastically reduce crop yield if not managed early.

Symptoms

  • Small, water-soaked lesions turning dark brown
  • Angular leaf spots bounded by veins
  • Dark streaks on stems and petioles
  • Boll rot that damages lint

Control & Cure

  • Use resistant cotton varieties
  • Avoid overhead irrigation
  • Use certified, disease-free seeds
  • Apply copper-based bactericides early
