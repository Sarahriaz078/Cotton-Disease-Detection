Army Worm Disease Information

Overview

Army worms are caterpillar pests that feed on cotton leaves, causing extensive defoliation. They travel in groups and can destroy crops rapidly if not controlled in time. Army worms are most active during the night.

Symptoms

  • Irregular holes or notches in leaves
  • Skeletonized leaves
  • Presence of caterpillars, especially under leaves
  • Rapid defoliation in patches
  • Reduced plant vigor and yield

Control & Cure

  • Use biological control like Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt)
  • Encourage natural predators such as birds and wasps
  • Apply specific insecticides early in the larval stage
  • Remove plant debris and maintain field hygiene
  • Conduct regular field monitoring
