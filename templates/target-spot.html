Target Spot Disease Information

Overview

Target Spot is a fungal disease caused by Corynespora cassiicola. It predominantly infects the leaves of cotton plants, reducing photosynthetic capacity and potentially causing significant defoliation and yield loss if not treated early.

Symptoms

  • Brown to reddish circular lesions with concentric rings
  • Symptoms start on lower leaves and move upward
  • Premature defoliation in severe cases
  • May resemble other foliar spot diseases

Control & Cure

  • Apply strobilurin or triazole-based fungicides early
  • Rotate crops to break fungal life cycles
  • Avoid overwatering; ensure good drainage
  • Provide adequate plant spacing for airflow
