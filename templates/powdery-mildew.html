Powdery Mildew Disease Information

Overview

Powdery mildew is a fungal disease caused by Leveillula taurica. It thrives in cool and humid environments, affecting the plant's photosynthesis process and leading to a decline in yield and crop quality.

Symptoms

  • White or gray powdery fungal growth on leaves
  • Yellowing and browning of infected leaves
  • Leaf curling and early leaf drop

Control & Cure

  • Use resistant or tolerant cotton varieties
  • Ensure good airflow and reduce crop humidity
  • Apply sulfur-based or systemic fungicides early
